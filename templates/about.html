03 : About

Background

I was born in Mexico in 1994. Like any mexican, I am passionate about our food, our drinks, and our culture.
In 2013, I moved to Vancouver to study biochemistry at the University of British Columbia.
In 2018, I travelled across the ocean to pursue a PhD in biochemistry at the University of Cambridge.

Research Interests

  • DNA Damage and Repair
  • CRISPR-Cas9 Systems
  • Bioinformatics and Data Science

My current research focuses on discovering mechanisms of resistance and hypersensitivity to future therapeutic treatments, unveiling novel genetic and functional interactions within the DNA Damage Response (DDR), and establishing the role of the tumour protein p53 in the performance of CRISPR-Cas9 screens.
I actively use Python and R to analyze my data and I am constantly expanding my programming skills.

Hobbies

I often play online chess, I am the captain of the graduate football team at my College, and I will always have time for a cold beer.
